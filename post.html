A new breed of content creators has emerged in today’s digital landscape. They are helping both brands in their marketing strategies and consumers in their purchase decisions—Generation Z!

Also known as Zoomers, Gen Z is an age group between the millennial cohort and generation Alpha. Gen Zers have become the most targeted demographic for companies and organizations worldwide. But on the flip side, influencers of this generation can be tapped by brands to help their businesses.

This page delves into Gen Z influencers as a new wave of digital content creators. Read on to understand what they are and how to engage with them to promote your business.

Influencer marketing has recently become all the rage. A form of social media marketing, it involves product placements and promotions from online content creators. According to Statista, its global market size has more than doubled since 2019, projected to hit $21.1 billion this year.

More than 50 million individuals now consider themselves influencers or digital content creators. On Instagram alone, there are over 64 million influencers worldwide. Statista also reported that TikTok has 106 influencers globally.

However, a powerful force has emerged in the business world—the Generation Z influencers. They have become prominent and influential on social media channels capable of impacting both brands and consumers.

Explore the rise of Gen Z influencers and learn the power they hold in uplifting your brand and shaping consumer behavior.

Who Is Gen Z?

Gen Z consists of individuals born between 1997 and 2012. They are those who succeeded the millennials. This generation has grown up with advanced technology, digital connectivity, and social media dominance.

According to Insense, we can sum up Generation Z as those who are:

  • Interested in the values and social impact of companies
  • Conscious and critical consumers in today’s business landscape
  • Employing digital capabilities to engage with brands

This generation has never experienced a world without the internet, smartphones, and social media platforms. Gen Zers are exposed to the digital landscape, allowing them to create and consume content across different platforms. As such, they are known for their digital expertise, often seen as tech-savvy, socially conscious, and entrepreneurial.
